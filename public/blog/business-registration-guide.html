How to Register a Business in Guwahati, Assam — Step by Step

Starting a business in Guwahati? Here's everything you need to know about choosing the right structure, required documents, and the registration process.

Step 1: Choose the Right Business Structure

Before registration, you must decide the legal structure. Each has different compliance requirements, tax implications, and liability exposure:

Sole Proprietorship

  • Best for: Solo entrepreneurs, freelancers, small shops
  • Registration: No formal registration required (use GST/MSME/Trade License as identity)
  • Liability: Unlimited personal liability
  • Compliance: Minimal — ITR filing, GST (if applicable)

Partnership Firm

  • Best for: Two or more partners with mutual trust
  • Registration: Partnership deed + optional Registrar of Firms registration
  • Liability: Unlimited, shared among partners

LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)

  • Best for: Professional services, consultants, small-medium businesses
  • Registration: Through MCA portal — LLP Agreement filing required
  • Liability: Limited to capital contribution
  • Compliance: Annual filing with ROC (Form 8, Form 11)

Private Limited Company

  • Best for: Startups seeking funding, scalable businesses
  • Registration: Through MCA — requires DSC, DIN, MOA, AOA
  • Liability: Limited to shareholding
  • Compliance: Highest — Board meetings, AGM, ROC filings, audit

Step 2: Required Registrations in Guwahati

Depending on your business type, you may need:

  • GST Registration — mandatory for turnover above ₹40L (goods) / ₹20L (services)
  • MSME/Udyam Registration — free, and unlocks government schemes
  • Trade License — required from Guwahati Municipal Corporation
  • Shop & Establishment Act — for commercial establishments in Assam
  • FSSAI License — for food-related businesses
  • Import Export Code (IEC) — for international trade
  • PAN & TAN — for tax deduction and filing

Step 3: Documents Required

  • PAN card of all promoters/partners
  • Aadhaar card
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Address proof of business premises (rent agreement + NOC, or electricity bill)
  • Bank account details
  • Digital Signature Certificate (for LLP/Company)
  • MOA/AOA or Partnership Deed (as applicable)

Step 4: Estimated Timeline

  • Proprietorship: 1-3 days (GST/MSME)
  • Partnership: 3-5 days
  • LLP: 7-15 days
  • Private Limited: 10-20 days

Step 5: Post-Registration Compliance

Registration is just the beginning. Ongoing compliance includes:

  • Monthly/quarterly GST return filing
  • Annual income tax return
  • ROC annual filing (for LLP/Company)
  • EPF/ESIC compliance (if you have employees)
  • TDS compliance
